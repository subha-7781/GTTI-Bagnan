Electrical Department

The George Telegraph Training Institute (GTTI) offers a diploma in electrical engineering course for students to enable them to work as electrical technicians. The course comprises the Electrical Technician and Armature Winder courses, both managed under the guidance of highly experienced faculty members.

On successful completion of any of the courses, recruitment assistance is also provided to the students. The Electrical Technician Course covers electrical theory and practical training, the outline of submersible pumps, engineering drawing, science, and calculation, etc. This course enables students to acquire jobs as electrical technicians in domestic or commercial electrification projects. They can also function as service technicians or electricians in factories and mines. Students will also have the basic knowledge to repair or rewind coils of damaged motors or make wooden forms and coils. The Armature Winder Course allows students to specialize in a critical area of electrical technology, such as repairing defective motor windings. Service technicians are highly in demand, and students completing this course will have many job opportunities open for them. Additionally, they can also set up their own service-providing business.

Electrical Technician

Eligibility: Appeared Class X Board Exam

Duration: 12 months

Course Outline:

  • Electrical Theory and Practical
  • Outline of Submersible Pump
  • Electronics Theory and Practical
  • Engineering Drawing
  • Science & Calculation
  • Overview on Armature Winder Theory & Practical

Work Areas:

After successful completion of this course, students would be able to work as electrical technicians and service technicians.

Armature Winder

Eligibility: Class IX

Duration: 06 months

Course Outline:

  • Trade Theory covering common tools, wires insulating materials, & varnishes, insulation papers, winding drawing, recording, etc.
  • Trade Practical covering single-phase fans, single-phase motors, 3-phase motors, single-phase transformers, wooden forms & coil width, armature, rewinding, fitting, & testing.

Work Areas:

After successful completion of this course, students would be able to work as service technicians or set up their own business.

SOLAR POWER TECHNOLOGY

Eligibility: Existing GTTI Students of Electrical, Electrical & Electronics Appliances

Duration: 02 months

Course Outline:

  • Basic Electrical Theory
  • Electrical Equipment: AC/DC Motor and Generator, Transformer, Different types of Starters, Equipment Protection, Relays, Cables, etc.
  • Basic Electronics: Diode, Transistor, FET, MOSFET, IGBT, SCR, GATE, SMPS, Inverter waveform, Deep Cycle Rechargeable Batteries, Solar PV panel, etc.

Work Areas:

Solar panel installation & maintenance, electronics department, system integration, and inverter installation & maintenance.
